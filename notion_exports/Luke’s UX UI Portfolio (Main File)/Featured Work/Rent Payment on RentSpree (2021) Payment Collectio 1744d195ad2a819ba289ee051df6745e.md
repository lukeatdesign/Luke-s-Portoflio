# Problem Statement

**The old way to pay rent has its problems in many aspects.**

Rental payment in US still has many underlying problems to their current journey such as lost check, pay in person, no dedicated app, etc.

**Rent Payment** is another flagship feature for RentSpree, the digital product that aim to simplify the rental process for every party.

<aside>

## My Role

- I joined RentSpree in March 2021, when the wireframes had already been published. I suggested some design changes before **taking ownership of the high-fidelity prototype work.**
- I frequently **discussed technical feasibilities** with developers and worked to align prioritized user stories with the Product Owner.
- I was responsible for **creating tickets for developers**, complete with user stories and acceptance criteria, and conducted **acceptance tests** to ensure that every task was thoroughly completed.
</aside>

# Integrate Prototyping with SCRUM

![image.png](Rent%20Payment%20on%20RentSpree%20(2021)%20Payment%20Collectio/image%201.png)

We always try to create the hi-fi design ahead of the development for 1-2 sprints to ensure **we are not too far ahead that some changes** will fold them all and still be in touch with the developers.

We find ways to improve the communication between developers and design during the development to **ensure the same page.** We create separate Figma project for designers to create the further design and align them according to the development sprint in another file.

![Picture13.png](Rent%20Payment%20on%20RentSpree%20(2021)%20Payment%20Collectio/Picture13.png)

We don’t aim for only specific devices but try to bring the **best experience and usability to every product.** We always tested each developed task on many devices and ensure if they work properly and ring the same usability.

We also serious about how can **we provide the good usage for most people**, concerning color set and enough contrast for many users so they can distinguish each status or what they can do in glances.

## **Bring the company ‘UX maturity’ to the next level.**

Most of times that I worked before I joined the RentSpree, the company or stakeholders are not aware of UX issues at all and it is very difficult to handle their expectations.

However, at RentSpree, most of the executives **care about user experience** even though they are not fully understand about it.

During last 3 months of my time at RentSpree, I was tasked with **promoting UX maturity across the company by initiating a revamp of the product design team's** structure. This included introducing **new positions and workflows** to keep pace with rapid development. I also served as a design reviewer, **ensuring that the interactions** between features adhered to our established visual and interaction guidelines.
